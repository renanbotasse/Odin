Odin Recipes: Cheese


Dairy products formed an important part of the diet in Viking Age Scandinavia. This is described in the Norse Creation Myth, and also the Icelandic sagas recount of cheesemaking. Archeaologists have found particular ceramics, which is likely to have been used in the process of cheesemaking.

Ingredients

  • 10 litres of unpasteurized milk
  • 5 litres of old-fashioned milk
  • cheese rennet
  • salt
  • brine
  • spices
  • mead
  • bowl
  • colander
  • towel
  • tablespoon

Steps

  1. Heat 10 litres of unpasteurized milk to 38 degrees celsius in two batches.
  2. pour 2 tablespoons of cheese rennet into each milk batch.
  3. When the rennet has been added the mix should be left to solidify for 30 minutes.
  4. To remove the whey from the curd the mass is poured into a colander that is rotated around the edge.
  5. The now fairly solid curd is placed in a towel and squeezed until all the whey is removed.
  6. Repeat the above with 5 litres of old-fashioned milk
  7. Add your desired spices and salt before the curd is completely dry.
  8. When the curd is ready it should be swept into a dry towel and placed in a mould.
  9. The towel should be changed daily. The cheese should be turned every morning and evening for at least 3-4 weeks.
  10. The cheese should be preserved for at least 5 weeks before it is ready to be eaten.
